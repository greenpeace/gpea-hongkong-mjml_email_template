In early August, Greenpeace released the results from our Supermarket Plastics Investigation Task Force, a collective effort of over 120 plastic-free volunteers.
 
We found 84% of the items examined were in disposable packaging, with 52% added by the supermarket after being shipped, some even added over the existing supplier packaging, after visited 100 supermarket outlets of 12 brands and investigated over 12,000 fresh produce and cooked foods items.
 
The appalling but unsurprising findings were immediately picked up by over 10 online news coverage, including this from scmp.com.
 
Together we can build pressure on the supermarkets by calling on them to reduce problematic and unnecessary plastic packaging. That's where you come in. Would you like to be a part of the growing movement in Hong Kong to #UnwrapOurGrocery?
 
 
Greenpeace is inviting at least 50 families to join a "7-day Plastic Challenge". All supermarket packaging you and your family members collect within a week will be audited. The data will then contribute to the calculation of household plastic waste originating from Hong Kong supermarkets.
 
Your effort will not end there. The plastic packaging collected will be upcycled. The mobile art installation will be an ambassador of our Plastic-Free Campaign in mid-October to call upon citizens to join our fight against plastic pollution and urge the supermarkets to go #PlasticFreeNow!
 
**7-day Plastic Challenge**

One cannot escape from plastic, scientific research tells us that microplastics can be traced from the Shing Mun River to Antarctica, from mullet to table salt. Melissa Wang from the Greenpeace Science Unit will be sharing her professional insights on the far-reaching harm of disposable plastic. You won't want to miss this face-to-face opportunity with one of our top Greenpeace scientists. We'll also be sharing progress on the multi-stakeholder forum Greenpeace Hong Kong's Plastic Team plans to hold later in October.
