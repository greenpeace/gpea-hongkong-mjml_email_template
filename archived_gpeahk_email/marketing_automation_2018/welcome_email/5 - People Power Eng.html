Dear Friends,

Hi, I’m James Ma, Direct Dialogue Fundraising Specialist at Greenpeace. I have been with Greenpeace for over 10 years now. Every day, I walk into different neighborhoods in Hong Kong, stand on the street and recruit members like you who are willing to donate to us. We spread green ideas and sow the seeds of conservation.

Can you believe it? I have introduced Greenpeace and its green mission to over 10,000 people! Are you one of them who have talked to me before?

 
 

A healthy environment for our kids

I support Greenpeace. My 7-year-old son is the reason behind. I want to pass on nature - unspoiled and clean - to the next generation. Let me tell you a secret: I donate to Greenpeace under his name. I always bring him to Greenpeace’s activities, practice what I preach. Hands-on action is how I teach him to love nature.

 

I believe that if everyone makes a small step and leads a green life, together we can spread our spirit across the globe and make a huge impact to save the environment! That’s why I’m not just a Greenpeace member or donor, but also an action volunteer! I’ve taken part in many of Greenpeace’s creative “non-violent direct actions”. This is how we garner public support and ripple out our impact.​

 

Who plays that “Lord Bao” in the picture?

In July 2012, in an action to call for a reform on the local electricity market, I dressed as “Lord Bao”. Lord Bao is a famous official in ancient China known for being just, impartial and vocal, fighting for the public. I presented an “Electricity Reform” proposal to Wong Kam-sing, the then newly appointed Secretary for the Environment. The proposal accused power companies of raising electricity bill in the name of “going green”.​

“Secretary Wong, don’t you kneel down before the rich and the privileged. Put a bridle on the two power companies and supervise them!” My preparation involved watching “Lord Bao” TV drama, imitating his demeanor. The hard work paid off as Secretary Wong cordially accepted the “Electricity Reform” order and the mission Lord Bao - which was me - conferred to him.​

 
 
 

The positive impact that Greenpeace has brought to the world is not a change that happened overnight. It was a tedious process of accumulation. Greenpeace started out as a small organization with very few supporters. But today there are more than 100,000 members in Greenpeace East Asia alone! By joining forces, we make the impossible possible.​

Greenpeace’s motto is “Positive Change through Action”. We invite you to be Greenpeace’s most durable shield, back us up and guard our wonderful environment together. Thank you for standing by our side once again!

James Ma
Direct Dialogue Fundraising Specialist
